Targeting Error Simulator for MRI-Guided Prostate Biopsy

Project Type:Graduate Project
Languages/Libraries:C++, VTK, ITK, KWWidgets
Software:Microsoft Visual Studio, CMake
Publications:Research Paper (SPIE 2010)
Research Paper (EMBS 2010)


The goal of this project was to develop a method to predict targeting error in an MRI-guided prostate biopsy procedure that is still in medical trials. The main deliverable was a simulator (written in C++) which takes in parameters for prostate shape, deformation, segmentation (i.e. contouring), and targeting. The output of the simulator is a "heat map" that gives a visual representation of the magnitude of the targeting errors along the surface of the prostate. The heat map and all prostate surfaces involved in the simulation are visible in real-time 3D.

Two conference papers have been published on this project. I was the first author on the first paper and the second author on the second paper. More information on the lab I worked at is available here.


What's going on in the video:

First part: This shows the targeting error "heat map", where red locations show unacceptable targeting errors (where the error is greater than the radius of the tumor), green locations show clinically insignificant targeting errors (where the error is less than the diameter of the needle), and the colors in between show where errors are in between these two thresholds.

Second part: This shows all the surfaces involved in one simulation. The blue points are the target points. Of the six surfaces on the left, the ones on the left show the pre-operative prostate, including the actual shape, the segmented surface and the difference between the two and the ones on the right show the same but for the intra-operative prostate. The next surface shows the resulting target locations to be used in the procedure while the final surface shows the difference with the actual intra-operative prostate.

The numbers on the bottom show the inputs of the simulation as well as relevant statistical data.
